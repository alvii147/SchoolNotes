Combinations & Permutations

Permutations without Repetition

Permutations without repetition refers to the number of arrangements formed by ordering elements from a pool of elements such that there are no repetitions.

Formula

Example

There are 12, 3-letter words that can be generated from the letters of the word FART:

FA, AF, FR, RF, FT, TF, AR, RA, AT, TA, RT, TR

Permutations with Repetition

Permutations with repetition refers to the number of arrangements formed by ordering elements from a pool of elements such that there may be repetitions.

Formula

Example

There are 8 possible arrangements of 1s and 0s in a 3-digit binary number:

000, 001, 010, 011, 100, 101, 110, 111.

Combinations without Repetition

Combinations without repetition refers to the number of ways to choose elements from a pool of elements such that there are no repetitions.

Formula

Example

There are 4, 3-letter combinations that can be generated from the letters of the word FART:

FAR, FRT, FAT, ART.

Combinations with repetition refers to the number of ways to choose elements from a pool of elements such that there may be repetitions.
