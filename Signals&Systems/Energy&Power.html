Energy Signals

The Average Energy Ex of a signal x(t) is defined as,

Ex=∫−∞∞|x(t)|2dt

If 0<Ex<∞, then x(t) is an Energy Signal.

Power Signals

The Average Power Px of a signal x(t) is defined as,

Px=limT→∞1T∫−T2T2|x(t)|2dt

If 0<Px<∞, then x(t) is a Power Signal.

NOTE: a signal cannot be both an energy and a power signal. It can, however, be neither.
