Discrete-Time Fourier Transform

Definition

Let x[n] be a discrete-time signal. Then,

DTFT{x[n]}=X(ejω)=∑n=−∞∞x[n]e−jωn

where DTFT{x[n]}=X(ejω) is the Discrete-Time Fourier Transform of x[n].

Conversely,

x[n]=12π∫−ππX(ejω)ejωndω

Note that the Discrete-Time Fourier Transform is obtained by restricting the Z-Transform to the unit circle on the complex plane.

Properties

Linearity

  • DTFT{c1x1[n]+c2x2[n]}=c1X1(ejω)+c2X2(ejω)

Time Advance

  • DTFT{x[n+k]}=ejkωX(ejω)

Time Delay

  • DTFT{x[n−k]}=e−jkωX(ejω)

Frequency Shifting

  • DTFT{ejω0nx[n]}=X(ej(ω−ω0))

Modulation

  • DTFT{x[n]cos(ω0n)}=12X(ej(ω+ω0))+12X(ej(ω−ω0))

Multiplication by n

  • DTFT{nx[n]}=−jdX(ejω)dω

Folding

  • DTFT{x[−n]}=X(e−jω)

Convolution

  • DTFT{x∗y}=X(ejω)Y(ejω)

Windowing

  • DTFT{x[n]w[n]}=12π∫2πX(ejθ)W(ej(ω−θ))dθ

Parseval's Theorem

  • ∑n=−∞∞x1[n]x2∗[n]=12π∫2πX1(ejω)X2∗(ejω)dω
  • ∑n=−∞∞|x[n]|2=12π∫2π|X(ejω)|2dω
