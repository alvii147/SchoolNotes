Discrete Fourier Transforms

Definition

Let x[n] be a discrete-time signal. Then,

X[k]=∑n=0N−1x[n]WNkn,0≤k<N

where WN=e−j2πN and X[k],0≤k<N are the Discrete Fourier Transform coefficients of x[n].

Conversely,

x[n]=1N∑k=0N−1X[k]WN−kn,0≤n<N

can be defined as the Inverse Discrete Fourier Transform to recover N samples of x[n].

Properties

Linearity

  • y[n]=c1x1[n]+c2x2[n]⟺c1X1[k]+c2X2[k]

Time-Reversal Circular Symmetry

  • y[n]=x[⟨−n⟩N]⟺Y[k]=X∗[k]

where x[⟨−n⟩N]=nmodN, and,

x[⟨−n⟩N]={x[0],n=0x[N−n],1≤n<N
  • x[n] is circular even ⟺x[n]=x[⟨−n⟩N]
  • x[n] is circular odd ⟺x[n]=−x[⟨−n⟩N]

Conjugation

  • y[n]=x∗[n]⟺X∗[⟨−k⟩N]

Duality

  • y[n]=X[n]⟺Nx[⟨−k⟩N]

Circular Time Shift

  • y[n]=x[⟨n−m⟩N]⟺Y[k]=WNmkX[k]

Circular Convolution

  • y[n]=(xNh)[n]⟺Y[k]=X[k]H[k]

where,

(xNh)[n]=∑m=0N−1h[m]x[⟨n−m⟩N],0≤n<N

Circular Correlation

  • rxy[n]=x[n]Ny[⟨−n⟩N]⟺R[k]=X[k]Y∗[k]

where,

rxy[l]=∑n=0N−1x[n]y[⟨n−l⟩N],0≤l<N

Frequency Shifting

  • y[n]=WN−mnx[n]⟺Y[k]=X[⟨k−m⟩N]

Frequency Modulation

  • y[n]=x[n]k0ncos⁡(2πN)⟺Y[k]=12X[⟨k+k0⟩N]+12X[⟨k−k0⟩N]

Windowing

  • y[n]=x[n]w[n]⟺1NX[k]NW[k]

Parseval's Theorem

  • y[n]=∑n=0N−1x[n]h∗[n]⟺Y[k]=1N∑k=0N−1X[k]Y∗[k]
  • y[n]=∑n=0N−1|x[n]|2⟺Y[k]=1N∑k=0N−1|X[k]|2
