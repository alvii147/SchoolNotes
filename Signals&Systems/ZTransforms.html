Z-Transforms

Definition

Let x[n] be a discrete-time signal. Then,

Z{x[n]}=∑k=0∞x[k]z−k

where Z{x[n]}=X(z) is the Z-Transform of x[n], assuming the sum converges.

Properties

Linearity

  • Z{c1x1[n]+c2x2[n]}=c1X1(z)+c2X2(z)

Time Advance

  • Z{x[n+k]}=zkX(z)

Time Delay

  • Z{x[n−k]}=z−kX(z)

Multiplication by n

  • Z{nx[n]}=−zdX(z)dz

Multiplication by an

  • Z{anx[n]}=X(zα)

Folding

  • Z{x[−n]}=X(1z)

Convolution

  • Z{x∗y}=X(z)Y(z)

Conjugation

  • Z{x∗[n]}=X∗(z∗)

Real & Imaginary Components

  • Z{Re{x[n]}}=12[X(z)+X∗(z∗)]
  • Z{Im{x[n]}}=12[X(z)−X∗(z∗)]

Initial Value Theorem

  • x[0]=limz→∞X(z)

Final Value Theorem

  • limn→∞x[n]=limz→1(z−1)X(z)

Condition: x[n] must converge to a finite value as n→∞.
